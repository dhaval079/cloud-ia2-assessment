Most Recently Used (MRU) Algorithm


Introduction:


The Most Recently Used (MRU) Algorithm is a page replacement algorithm used in operating systems to manage memory. It replaces the most recently used page when a new page needs to be brought into memory and the memory is full. MRU prioritizes retaining the pages that have been accessed most recently.


Characteristics of Most Recently Used (MRU) Algorithm:


  • Replaces the most recently used page in memory.
  • Does not consider the frequency of page accesses, only the most recent access.
  • Suitable for scenarios where recent data is more likely to be accessed again.
  • Simple and straightforward implementation.

Advantages of Most Recently Used (MRU) Algorithm:


  • Efficient for scenarios where recently accessed pages are likely to be accessed again soon.
  • Minimal overhead in implementation.
  • Straightforward logic and easy to understand.

Disadvantages of Most Recently Used (MRU) Algorithm:


  • May lead to poor performance in scenarios where recent accesses are not indicative of future accesses.
  • Does not consider the frequency of page accesses, which may result in inefficient memory usage.
  • Not suitable for all types of workload patterns.

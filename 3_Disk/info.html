Disk Scheduling Algorithm


Introduction:


Disk Scheduling Algorithms are methods used to order or prioritize the access of data on a disk drive. These algorithms aim to minimize the seek time (time taken to position the disk arm to the desired track) and optimize the overall performance of disk operations.


Characteristics of Disk Scheduling Algorithm:


  • Minimization of seek time is a primary objective.
  • Efficiently manages disk I/O requests.
  • Various algorithms like FCFS, SSTF, SCAN, C-SCAN, LOOK, and C-LOOK are used.
  • Adaptability to different workloads and system configurations.

Advantages of Disk Scheduling Algorithm:


  • Improved disk access time.
  • Optimized disk performance.
  • Effective utilization of disk resources.
  • Compatibility with different disk systems.

Disadvantages of Disk Scheduling Algorithm:


  • May lead to starvation for certain I/O requests in some algorithms.
  • Increased complexity in managing disk operations.
  • Difficulty in predicting optimal scheduling for dynamic workloads.
  • Overhead in implementing and maintaining scheduling algorithms.
